Hello, World!

Blogging like it's the '90s

“Hey, while you’re working on that side project, maybe you should blog about it?”

Fateful words spoken to me while I was describing a little idea, working title ‘matchymatchy’, to a friend at work… and now here we are! I’m planning on using this blog as a dev-diary of sorts to help keep a record of my experience building out this project and the various decisions, big and small, I make along the way.

But first, a little intro: Hi! I’m Michael, a backend software developer based in London. I first started programming a little under four years ago as a fresh-faced graduate for a company called Metaswitch, based just North of London. I spent nearly three years there, learning the ins and outs of systems programming in C, scripting of test frameworks in Python and, in my last couple of months, backend web development in Scala for which I gained a real passion.

The application I helped create as my first foray into Scala and web development was a configuration and management API serving a client another team created. I loved the technical aspects of that project - agreeing interfaces that lived up to RESTful best practise and figuring out creative ways to improve performance when interacting with an underlying legacy configuration system - and wanted to do more of it when active development was wound down.

To that end, I set off into the world of startups, SaaS and Scala. I joined Goodlord as a backend developer in June 2017 and was thrown into a world of ‘Back end’ and ‘Front end’ developers - a far cry from the universal ‘Software Engineer’ titles at Metaswitch, and the systems-style that implied. Since then I’ve been playing nonstop buzzword bingo with a team of fantastic, experienced web developers - React, Yarn, Babel, Webpack, Gulp, Emotion… the list goes on. Hopefully completing a full-stack project of my own will help me come to terms with, and maybe even master, some of them. Why don’t you come along for the ride?
